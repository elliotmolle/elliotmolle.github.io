About Me

I’m an Electrical Engineering student at Texas A&M '27 with a hands-on focus in power electronics. I like building real hardware—designing and testing converters, characterizing devices, and validating ideas on the bench with scopes and probes. My goal is to help connect renewables, storage, and modern loads to the grid with scalable, efficient power conversion.

I learn by doing. Recent builds include a small generator system (hoverboard wheel alternator → rectifier → switching stage with diode protection → battery charging → buck step-down → USB breakout) and a repurposed wall-mounted monitor from a salvaged laptop panel with an LCD controller. Projects like these sharpen my component selection, safe lab practice, and troubleshooting skills while reinforcing first-principles thinking.

Day to day, I split time between bench work and quick simulations to sanity-check behavior. I enjoy collaborating, documenting results clearly, and iterating until the hardware performs the way the math says it should.
